About Uq Lyfe

A number of university students aren’t involved with on-campus activities and social events within their campus. The cause for this lack of engagement can come down to a variety of issues, such as a la ck of personal motivation or a lack of awareness. UQ Lyfe, is a mobile application that gamifies the extracurricular experience, as a way to incentivize students who wouldn’t normally be involved.

The UQ Lyfe system is broken up into several tags and ordered by the amount of experience points gained from the completion, which contributes to the overall rank associated with the user’s profile. Experience can be earned through various tasks such as attending events, completing tasks, hosting events and having students attend the events you have created. Badges can also be gained through completing specific tasks, encouraging students to become active within their community.

UQ Lyfe introduces:

  • Sense of community with instant messaging, school factions and leaderboards
  • Create a shared information space to provide a centralised system for UQ events
  • Increase awareness within the community by representing the students surroundings
  • Introduce a new form of context for UQ students by creating the badge system
  • Establish a social mobile environment to facilitate direct interactions

Design Process

The development of UQ Lyfe was broken into four distinct stages. This was to take advantage of the deliverable standups, to receive heuristic advice from our teachers who are experts within the problem space. They were also short cycles, fitting within the Agile framework.

As a team, we rapidly adapted the product once unforeseen user requirements arose, using test sessions to guide the design. This encouraged feedback and nurtured collaborative design. Including users in the decision making process, they had a sense of ownership into the project and made UQ Lyfe highly appropriate to the users within the communities problem space.
